Design by the Decade:
What the Fortune 500 Tells Us About the Best Logos of All Time

In this story, we will determine the top corporate logos of all time based on Fortune 500 lists published since 1955. You will be able to explore the history of logos and learn about corporate design trends from each decade since the 1950s.

During WWII, Americans had put all efforts towards the war, bringing American production and manufacturing to a halt. In the 1950s, Americans were flush with cash and businesses expanded production, bringing about an unprecedented era of American consumerism. The rapid development of technology made it harder for companies to beat their competition with high-quality products alone, so this decade forced companies to distinguish themselves by creating a brand identity and reputation. With this rise in technology, television was also becoming a staple in the American household, and companies saw this as an opportunity for advertising.

The logos of the 1950s represent Modernism with sans-serif type, primary colors, and simple, geometric forms or symbols. Design was simplified through the integration of text and image into overlapping forms. American idealism in this decade led to experimentation in logo design, as designers sought to create logos that could work in various types of media and that could be recognized more quickly as the world picked up its pace after the war. Despite the rise in television, print media was still the primary source of information for Americans, so many logos remained without gradients, shadows, or intricate details that would be hard to print.
